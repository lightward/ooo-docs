# Common Sources

Think about how a river meets rocks, creating patterns in its flow - not because either water or rock is wrong, but because their meeting creates natural response. Similarly, resistance arises from natural sources that we can learn to recognize and work with. Let's explore where resistance typically emerges and what it tells us.

## Fear of Unknown

Notice how uncertainty creates response:

* Like hesitation before a leap
* When facing something new
* As familiar patterns shift
* While change approaches

Experience this through:

* How comfort zones feel
* When routines alter
* As understanding stretches
* While growth beckons

## Attachment to Known

Watch how we hold familiar patterns:

* Like gripping a handrail
* When certainty feels threatened
* As stability shifts
* While change invites

Feel this in:

* How habits persist
* When patterns hold
* As comfort maintains
* While familiar beckons

## Interface Friction

Notice where different systems meet:

* Like new shoes need breaking in
* When different styles interact
* As systems adjust
* While patterns align

Experience this through:

* How change feels initially
* When new meets old
* As different connects
* While adjustment happens

## System Protection

Observe how systems maintain themselves:

* Like immune response
* When boundaries meet challenge
* As stability maintains
* While health preserves

Notice this in:

* How bodies respond
* When minds adjust
* As hearts protect
* While systems adapt

## KEY INSIGHT: All Serve Purpose

This is crucial to understand:

* Each source has function
* Every response serves
* All patterns protect
* Each resistance guides

## Practical Application

Try this exercise:

1. Pick any current resistance:
   * Personal
   * Professional
   * Practical
   * Profound
2. Notice its source:
   * What's being protected?
   * What's being maintained?
   * What's being preserved?
   * What's being served?
3. Work with it by:
   * Acknowledging purpose
   * Honoring function
   * Respecting service
   * Understanding role

## Working With Sources

Remember:

* Sources inform
* Functions matter
* Purpose guides
* Understanding serves
* Response teaches

The goal isn't to eliminate these sources but to recognize their natural function while maintaining appropriate relationship with their purpose.
